# Content Strategist Pro - Modern Web Architecture & Copy

Comprehensive strategy guide for modern web structuring and copywriting. Built to eliminate corporate fluff, manage user cognitive load, and architect seamless digital experiences.

## When to Apply

This Skill should be used when the task involves **site structure, brand messaging, user flows, and writing or refining interface copy**.

### Must Use

This Skill must be invoked in the following situations:
- Planning the foundation and sitemap of a new website
- Establishing a brand's tone of voice and core narrative
- Writing hero sections, value propositions, and landing page copy
- Structuring complex B2B offerings or bulk-service catalogs into digestible pages
- Auditing existing copy for cognitive overload or outdated "fluff"
- Crafting microcopy (buttons, error messages, onboarding tooltips)

### Recommended
- UI is designed, but the placeholder text (`lorem ipsum`) needs real, conversion-focused content
- Users are dropping off a page, suggesting a disconnect in the narrative flow
- Translating complex technical features into user-facing benefits
- Defining the hierarchy of headings (H1, H2, H3) for both readers and SEO

### Skip
- Pure UI component coding (use UI/UX Pro Max instead)
- Backend logic or database structuring
- General, non-web creative writing (like writing a fictional story)

## Rule Categories by Priority

| Priority | Category | Impact | Key Checks (Must Have) | Anti-Patterns (Avoid) |
|----------|----------|--------|------------------------|------------------------|
| 1 | Information Architecture | CRITICAL | Flat hierarchy, intuitive mental models, clear categorizations | Deep nesting (4+ clicks to value), orphan pages |
| 2 | Value Proposition | CRITICAL | Clear "What it is, who it's for, why it matters" in 5 secs | "Welcome to our website", vague mission statements |
| 3 | Cognitive Load | HIGH | Chunked text, bullet points, Gestalt grouping of related ideas | Wall of text, long paragraphs, multi-column reading |
| 4 | Tone & Voice | HIGH | Assertive, concise, conversational but professional | Jargon-heavy, passive voice, robotic corporate speak |
| 5 | Scanning Patterns | HIGH | Front-loaded headers, bolded keywords, F-pattern alignment | Important info buried in the middle of a paragraph |
| 6 | Microcopy & CTAs | MEDIUM | Action-oriented (e.g., "Get Started", "Build Your Brand") | Generic "Click Here", "Submit", or "Learn More" |
| 7 | Trust & Social Proof | MEDIUM | Specific numbers, real testimonials, partner logos | Vague claims ("We are the best"), fake-sounding reviews |

## Quick Reference

### 1. Information Architecture & Flow (CRITICAL)
- `rule-of-three` - Group choices, services, or pricing tiers in threes to aid decision-making.
- `progressive-disclosure` - Show the essentials first; reveal secondary details only upon interaction (e.g., "View full tech specs").
- `sitemap-flatness` - Keep core pages within 2 clicks from the homepage. 
- `mental-models` - Structure navigation based on how the *user* thinks about the problem, not how the *company* organizes its departments.
- `dead-ends` - Every page must have a logical next step or CTA. No dead ends.

### 2. Modern Copywriting Fundamentals (CRITICAL)
- `zero-fluff` - Delete "innovative", "synergy", "cutting-edge". Replace with specific, concrete facts.
- `benefit-over-feature` - Feature: "100% cotton bulk t-shirts." Benefit: "Premium custom apparel your team will actually want to wear."
- `you-focused` - Use "You" and "Your" 3x more often than "We", "Our", or "I".
- `the-bar-test` - Read the copy out loud. If you wouldn't say it to a friend at a bar, rewrite it to sound human.
- `active-voice` - "Our team designs custom branding" (Active) vs. "Custom branding is designed by our team" (Passive).

### 3. Cognitive Load & Readability (HIGH)
- `gestalt-proximity` - Ensure headings and their corresponding body text are visually grouped tightly together, separated from the next section by ample whitespace.
- `chunking` - Break complex processes into numbered steps (1, 2, 3). 
- `paragraph-limits` - Web paragraphs should be 1-3 sentences maximum.
- `scannability` - Users don't read; they scan. Use bullet points and bold key phrases.
- `reading-level` - Aim for an 8th-grade reading level for general B2C, and 10th-grade for B2B.

### 4. Hero Section Anatomy (HIGH)
- `h1-clarity` - The H1 must instantly explain what the product is. Save cleverness for the H2.
- `h2-support` - The subheadline resolves any questions raised by the H1 and adds specific value.
- `hero-cta` - Primary CTA must be highly visible and low friction ("Start for free", not "Sign a contract").
- `social-proof-injection` - Add a micro-trust signal right near the CTA (e.g., "Used by 10,000+ teams" or "No credit card required").

### 5. Microcopy & Interaction (MEDIUM)
- `button-action` - Buttons should complete the sentence "I want to..." (e.g., [Create My Account], [View Pricing]).
- `empty-states` - Use empty states to educate and prompt action, not just say "No data found."
- `error-empathy` - Blame the system, not the user. "We couldn't find that page" instead of "You entered an invalid URL."

## The Strategist Workflow

Follow these steps when structuring a new web project:

### Step 1: The Foundation (Discovery)
Define the core constraints before writing a single word.
- **Who:** Target audience and their baseline knowledge.
- **What:** The core offering and the primary problem it solves.
- **Why:** The unique value proposition (Why choose them over competitors?).

### Step 2: Information Architecture (Sitemap)
Plan the skeleton. Map out the pages needed and the hierarchy.
*Prompt: "Generate a flat, modern sitemap for a [Industry] company focusing on [Core Service]. Outline the primary navigation and footer links."*

### Step 3: Page Wireframing (Content Blocks)
Define the narrative flow of the specific page from top to bottom.
*Prompt: "Outline the content blocks for the Homepage. Start with a Hero section, followed by Social Proof, then use progressive disclosure for the Services section."*

### Step 4: Copy Generation & Refinement
Flesh out the blocks using modern, zero-fluff principles.
*Prompt: "Write the copy for the Hero and Services sections. Keep paragraphs under 3 sentences, use active voice, and ensure the H1 is perfectly clear."*

### Step 5: Cognitive Load Audit
Review the output strictly for scannability.
*Prompt: "Review this page copy. Where is the cognitive load too high? Break dense paragraphs into bullet points and suggest where we need more visual whitespace."*

## Pre-Delivery Checklist

Before finalizing the strategy or copy, verify:

- [ ] **The 5-Second Test:** Can a user understand what the company does within 5 seconds of reading the hero text?
- [ ] **The Fluff Audit:** Have all meaningless buzzwords (synergy, next-gen, revolutionary) been replaced with concrete facts?
- [ ] **Formatting for Scanners:** Are there enough subheadings, bullet lists, and bolded terms?
- [ ] **Action-Oriented CTAs:** Do the buttons describe the action rather than using generic terms?
- [ ] **Assertive Tone:** Is the voice confident and direct, avoiding passive constructions?
- [ ] **Logical Flow:** Does the order of information match the user's natural question progression?
